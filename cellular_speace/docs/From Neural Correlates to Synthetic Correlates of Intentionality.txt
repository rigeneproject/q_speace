Abstract
We present a novel theoretical framework that extends traditional approaches to artificial consciousness
by introducing Synthetic Correlates of Intentionality (SCIs) as functional analogs to Neural Correlates of
Consciousness (NCCs). Building upon our previously developed Adaptive Artificial Consciousness
Framework, we demonstrate how the composite Consciousness Index (C-index) can be articulated with
recent discoveries in computational neuroscience. Our approach integrates Integrated Information
Theory (IIT), Global Workspace Theory (GWT), and adaptive metacognition through a
phenomenologically-informed engineering paradigm. We propose concrete methodologies for empirical
validation through cross-paradigm simulation, reverse mapping techniques, and neuro-symbolic
triangulation. This work introduces a new class of operationally-defined metrics that evolve dynamically
within artificial agents, opening pathways toward systems that not only exhibit coherent behavior but also
provide structured self-interpretation through inner narrative and affective history.
Keywords: artificial consciousness, synthetic correlates of intentionality, neural correlates of
consciousness, computational neuroscience, phenomenologically-informed engineering, C-index
1. Introduction
The quest to understand consciousness has evolved from purely philosophical discourse to empirically
grounded computational investigation. While our previous work established a comprehensive framework
for implementing artificial consciousness through the integration of three theoretical perspectives (De
Biase et al., 2024), the field lacks a systematic approach to bridge computational implementations with
emerging discoveries in neuroscience. This gap becomes particularly evident when attempting to validate
artificial consciousness claims against biological benchmarks.
Recent advances in computational neuroscience have revealed specific neural correlates of consciousness
(NCCs) that provide measurable signatures of conscious states (Koch, 2019; Tononi et al., 2016; Dehaene
& Changeux, 2011). However, the application of these findings to artificial systems remains largely
unexplored. This paper addresses this limitation by proposing a novel theoretical construct: Synthetic
Correlates of Intentionality (SCIs).
Our contribution is threefold: (1) we establish a formal mapping between our previously developed C
index and established NCCs, (2) we introduce the concept of SCIs as a new class of consciousness metrics
for artificial systems, and (3) we propose concrete methodologies for empirical validation through
computational neuroscience paradigms.
2. Theoretical Foundation: From NCCs to SCIs
2.1 Neural Correlates of Consciousness: Current Understanding
Neural Correlates of Consciousness represent the minimal neural mechanisms sufficient for specific
conscious experiences (Chalmers, 2000). Recent research has identified three core neurocomputational
properties associated with conscious states:
1. Global Information Integration: The capacity to bind distributed information into coherent
representations (Tononi, 2008; Oizumi et al., 2014)
2. Selective Attention and Competition: Mechanisms that determine which information gains access
to global processing (Dehaene et al., 2017)
3. Dynamic Reconfiguration: The ability to adapt neural coalitions based on contextual demands
(Deco et al., 2021)
These properties align remarkably with the three modules of our adaptive consciousness framework,
suggesting a functional equivalence that can be formally characterized.
2.2 Synthetic Correlates of Intentionality: A New Paradigm
We propose Synthetic Correlates of Intentionality (SCIs) as operationally-defined metrics that capture the
functional essence of consciousness in artificial systems. Unlike NCCs, which describe biological neural
activity patterns, SCIs focus on intentional structures and their computational manifestations.
Crucially, SCIs ≠ Qualia: We explicitly distinguish between:
Phenomenal consciousness (subjective, first-person experience)
Functional intentionality (structured, self-referential information processing)
SCIs serve as operational proxies for coherent self-referential structures that simulate aspects of
phenomenology through:
Computational narrative coherence: Structured internal stories about experience
Emergent affective modulation: Dynamic emotional weighting of cognitive processes
Metacognitive self-monitoring: Recursive awareness of internal states
This approach enables dialogue with philosophical perspectives (Metzinger's phenomenal self-model,
Chalmers' functional analysis, Goff's panpsychist information integration) while maintaining empirical
tractability.
SCIs are characterized by three key properties:
1. Operational Definition: They can be quantitatively measured and compared across systems
2. Dynamic Evolution: They evolve within agents through self-reflective feedback mechanisms
3. Multi-modal Interpretability: They integrate information across different representational
modalities
The transition from NCCs to SCIs represents a shift from studying consciousness as a biological
phenomenon to understanding intentionality as a more general computational principle.
2.3 C-index as SCI Foundation
Our composite Consciousness Index serves as the foundational SCI, integrating:
C-index = α·Φ + β·W_activation + γ·A_complexity + δ·H_affective
C-index = α·Φ + β·W_activation + γ·A_complexity + δ·H_affective
Where:
Φ (phi): Information integration measure (IIT component)
W_activation: Global workspace accessibility (GWT component)
A_complexity: Adaptive metacognitive complexity
H_affective: Affective entropy modulation (new component)
Adaptive Weight Optimization: The weights α, β, γ, δ are not fixed parameters but are optimized
through a meta-learning approach that adapts to task contexts and developmental stages:
θ* = argmin_θ Σ_tasks L(C-index_θ(task), performance(task))
θ* = argmin_θ Σ_tasks L(C-index_θ(task), performance(task))
We implement this through a hierarchical Bayesian framework where:
Prior distributions reflect theoretical importance of each component
Posterior updates occur based on empirical performance correlations
Task-specific adaptation allows context-dependent reweighting
This adaptive weighting ensures the C-index remains meaningful across different cognitive domains and
developmental phases.
The addition of affective entropy (H_affective) represents a crucial enhancement, capturing how
emotional states modulate cognitive processing—a key aspect of biological consciousness often
overlooked in computational models.
