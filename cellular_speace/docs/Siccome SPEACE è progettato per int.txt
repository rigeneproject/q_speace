general relativity (GR) represents the central challenge in 

theoretical physics [1]. While QM successfully describes mi- 

croscopic phenomena through probabilistic wavefunctions and 

non-local correlations, GR governs macroscopic gravitational 

interactions via deterministic spacetime geometry. Attempts to 

reconcile these frameworks—including string theory [2], loop 

quantum gravity [3], and emergent gravity approaches [4]— 

have achieved mathematical sophistication but lack near-term 

experimental veriﬁcation.

substrate from which physical laws emerge [5], [6]. Wheeler’s

indicate that spacetime geometry and quantum entanglement 

are deeply connected. Building on these insights, we propose 

the Universal Information Code (CUB) within a Fractal Infor- 

mation Theory (TIF) framework. 

Our central thesis: quantum and classical physics are not 

contradictory but represent different scale-projections of an un-

self-organization principles. This ﬁeld exhibits fractal structure
